**Definition:**

Cameras are optical instruments for capturing or recording images.

How a Camera Works: A camera uses its lens, a curved piece of plastic or glass and takes in any light beams bouncing off an object. These light waves are redirected together to form an image.

**Characteristics of Cameras:**

**1. Passive Sensing:** A camera is a passive sensing system, which is in contrast to an active sensing system. This means that a camera is able to sense its surroundings without emitting any waves of its own.

**2. 2D Imagery:** Produces images in 2D that often are converted into 3D models.

**3. Range is dependent on conditions:** A camera operates on reflected light. Therefore, it does not operate well in adverse weather conditions or at night.

**4. Superior Classification and Texture Interpretation:** Cameras are the only sensor that can capture texture, color and contrast information, making them crucial for classification.

**5. Low Cost:** Cameras are by far the cheapest and highly available of the sensors.

**6. Computationally Intense:** Cameras generate massive amounts of data due to the high resolution HD videos/images that are processed.

**Current Camera Usage in Advanced Driver-Assistance Systems (ADAS)**

**1. Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC):** currently consistently detect full-width vehicles like cars and trucks, these need to be able to classify a motorcycle and keep distance.

**2. Automatic High Beam Control (AHBC):** currently do high-low beam switching and need to evolve to be able to detect oncoming vehicle(s) and contour the ray of light accordingly.

**3. Traffic Sign Recognition (TSR):** current systems recognize speed limits and various limited subset of signs. Future systems need to understand supplemental signs and context, (Speed limit in effect 10am to 8pm”) detect traffic signals to adapt ACC , stop, slow down etc.

**4. Lane Keep Systems (LKS):** currently detect lane markings, future systems need to detect drivable surface, adapt to construction signs and multiple lane markings.
